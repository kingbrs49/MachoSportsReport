Kings Break Losing Streak w/o Boogie: Rapid Reaction

by Paulo C Camacho

February 2, 2016




The Sacramento Kings were in the midst of a losing streak that was about to offset their season-high five-game winning streak from a week ago. They also lost perennial All-Star DeMarcus Cousins to a sprained ankle in their previous game against the Memphis Grizzlies.

Against a young, energetic Bucks team at Sleep Train Arena, they really needed a win that was in no way a sure thing.

Thanks to a late push by Rajon Rondo and Marco Bellinelli - and a game-high 32-point performance by Rudy Gay - the Kings broke a four-game losing streak in a see-saw battle with Milwaukee, 111-104.

Before their game against the Bucks, the Kings were 1-7 without their franchise big man, who had been averaging 27.0 points and 11.3 rebounds per game in 39 games played.

Their lone win without Cousins in the lineup? At Milwaukee, against the Bucks.

This time around, Gay picked up the slack, going 13-for-20 from the field, and 1-for-3 from behind the arc. Rondo added 19 points, including an uncharacteristic 4-for-4 from three-point land, while Belinelli contributed 18 points in 36 minutes of play.



Omri Casspi (13 points) and Darren Collison (14 points) made a huge difference off the bench for the Kings (21-27), outscoring Milwaukee's reserves by 12.

Milwaukee (20-30) kept the game competitive throughtout, leading by as much as 8 late in the first half. Greg Monroe led a starting lineup that finished all five players scoring in double digits, ending up with 24 points and 12 rebounds in a losing effort.


Notes:

  • The Kings continue to surprise - this time, however, pleasantly so. After all, without their big man, the question was where the offensive production was going to come from. Gay Answered the call, in only his second game back from an eye contusion suffered in the Kings' double-overtime loss to Charlotte kept him out of his next two games. It was a gut-check game for Gay, who had not scored over 30 points in a game since January 5th, in a double-overtime loss to Dallas. Whether this helps him in his long-term plans to stay in Sacramento, remains to be seen. Rumors abound that he could be on the trading block, regardless of his production moving forward.

  • Their next five games will test the Kings' mettle, especially if Cousins sits out for an extended period of time. They play at home against Chicago on Wednesday, who have lost their two, but still pose a challenge in guard Jimmy Butler and power forward Pau Gasol. After that, they face a four-game road trip, facing the Cleveland Cavaliers and Boston Celtics. And, considering their penchant for falling into trap games, even with their talented center, the Kings cannot overlook the Brooklyn Nets (12-37) or the Philadelphia 76ers (7-41) - the latter of which took the Golden State Warriors to the wire at home.
