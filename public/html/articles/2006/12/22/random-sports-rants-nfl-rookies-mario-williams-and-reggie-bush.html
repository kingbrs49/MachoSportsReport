Random Sports Rants: NFL Rookies, Mario Williams & Reggie Bush



This rant was recorded , by myself, at around 4:30 am, in the lobby of a La Quinta inn in Austin, Texas, on July 30th, 2006. It took place after Vernon Davis of the 49ers signed a record deal for a tight end, at the end of July. Read and enjoy, sports fans:

All of the Niners picks are signed. That’s nice to know. Apparently, Vernon Davis will now be the highest-paid tight end in the league, “and he hasn’t played a down in the National Football League as of yet,” so say most of the sports media. It seems like that’s an important aspect to point out whenever there are high rookie contracts. It’s so important for sports writers to point that out whenever some rookie gets a record deal. “They haven’t proven anything in the pros,” they usually say. Well, duh!! (I know, I know, not a professional phrase to use in journalism, but hey, give me a break, it’s 5 in the morning.) That’s why they are rookies! The point is, they show so much potential to be great in the pros, the money they get is warranted.

Take Alex Smith ... no, bad example ...

Take Mario Williams – best defensive player, by far, in this year’s draft, and (despite Houston’s reservations) number one overall pick in the draft, he got paid – 25 million up front money. Had he played a down yet? No! But he’s gonna get 25 mil because he was the number one pick, and talent taken number one warrants that kind of money. It’s the nature of the business. Look, the point is, top-flight rookies have to start somewhere, and pro teams wouldn’t take them so high if they didn’t consider them so highly-regarded in talent. So, obviously, they have to start high when it comes to contracts. Again – nature of the business.

By the way – it’s about time Reggie Bush signed with the Saints. Six-year deal, I hear. Good for him. I might address this later, if I feel like it. You know, I still can’t get over the fact that the Texans passed on him. Was it the house thing? Because, honestly, if it was, it was a foolish reason to talk oneself out of the best running back since LaDainian Tomlinson with Gale Sayers talent. It’s just insane.

If I were Charley Casserley, I wouldn’t care if the dude got 10 houses “under suspicious circumstances” – the boy can play. Houston needed offense – it needed a spark plug to boost the poor excuse for an offense it shells out every week for the Texans. Can you imagine the Colts, Titans and Jaguars dealing with Reggie Bush year in and year out? Dude’s a four-tier player – he can be a running back, split to wideout, return kicks and punts. He’s the secret weapon that can elude almost anyone.

I am not going to sit here and knock Mario Williams – that dude is a monster. He could be the next Reggie White, the style of play he has. He has the potential to stuff guys like Manning, Young and Leftwich for years to come. I understand that Houston was lacking everywhere, but dammit! You had the chance to draft the most dangerous weapon to come along in 20 years, and you pass that up because you’re afraid you won’t get a defensive player unless you sign Williams? Look, I’m sorry, there is not one compelling reason out there to convince me that Houston had to take Williams over Bush.

You look at the potential damage Bush can do to the rest of the AFC South – it far outweighs the potential Williams can do on opposing offenses. It’s also unfair to Williams, if you really look at it – forever, Williams will be looking in the rearview, checking on Bush’s career, and seeing if his stacks up evenly. If Bush goes for 1300 on the ground, 900 in the air, 600 return yards and helps the Saints reach the playoffs, while he has 8 sacks, 150 total tackles and Houston doesn’t make it, he’s gonna catch hell, because he didn’t do for the Texans what Bush did for New Orleans – it’s totally unfair, but Houston put him in that position.
