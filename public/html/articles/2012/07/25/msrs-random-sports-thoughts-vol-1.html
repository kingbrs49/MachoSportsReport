MSR’s Random Sports Thoughts, Vol. 1

by Paulo C Camacho

July 25, 2012



The MLB Trade Deadline is next week, and the Giants could make some moves – after all, All-Star 3B Pablo Sandoval recently injured his hamstring making a play against the Padres a couple nights ago.

With no word on how long he’ll be out, San Francisco brass may want to plug a free agent bat in the lineup to replace him, just in case. However, if that’s the official strategy, they may need to rethink it: the Black-and-Orange traded for slugger Carlos Beltran this time last season, and the move ended up costing them a prized pitching prospect, while offering little help down the stretch in the division race.



Better to stay the course, see if they can get a pickup on waivers, and hope everyone gets healthy – and right (I’m looking at you, Tim Lincecum) – come late September.

I think it’s odd that the Olympics officially begin a few days before the opening ceremony – and I’m not even referring to USA Men’s Basketball, as they have been on an exhibition tour.

Apparently, Group stages in women’s soccer began today, with the United States coming back from an early two-goal deficit against France to take the victory, 4-2.

Considering how close the women’s team got to winning the World Cup last July, you would think that NBC Sports would be hyping up the opening match like gangbusters. The only reason I knew about it was a headline on ESPN.com – and that was well into the second half.

Did I miss something, or did sports media simply drop the ball?

On that same note, it seemed like the Women’s National Team suffered a bit of a hangover from last July’s WWC loss – falling behind 2-0 to fellow Women’s World Cup semifinalist France in the first 14 minutes.

Despite the deficit, mainstays Abby Wambach, Carli Lloyd and Alex Morgan scored four unanswered goals, with Morgan equaling her WWC performance vs. France, by knocking in two. Goalkeeper Hope Solo blanked out the French the rest of the way, for an impressive, yet dicey, victory.

The episode speaks to not only their precociousness as Olympic favorites, but also to their dominance.



The Dodgers acquired Miami Marlins 3B Hanley Ramirez today – both of which have been struggling of late.

While Los Angeles has gone a paltry 21-30 since their sizzling 32-15 start, Ramirez – a star player with the Marlins for the past seven years – has had one of the least productive seasons in his career. Their combined forces are meant to rejuvenate each other, and give themselves a fighting chance against division-leading San Francisco.

There’s a 50-50 chance this could either work out well for both parties, or the experiment could blow up in their faces.

You can only guess which scenario I’m hoping for.

Look, I commend pro athletes for having the intestinal fortitude (read: BAWLS) to make bold proclamations, but do they have to do it via the media?

First, it was Philadelphia Eagles QB Michael Vick – apparently not learning from former teammate Vince Young, and his rants about an Eagles “Dream Team” – who was recently quoted as saying that the Eagles were building a dynasty.

Then – and, sort of out of left field, mind you – Carolina Panthers center Ryan Kalil bought a full-page ad, proclaiming to their fanbase and NFL Nation that they would win this year’s Super Bowl.

Way I see it, proclamations of this kind are a no-win situation for the person who makes it, and the team he/she is affiliated with. More likely that person will be wrong, so why put themselves in that position in the first place?

Personally, I just think it’s useless rhetoric. Furthermore, it just makes that person look like a jackass.

Speak softly, and carry a big stick, my friends.
