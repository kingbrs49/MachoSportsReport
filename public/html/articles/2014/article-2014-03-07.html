49ers Eyeing Local Standout from TCU



Sit back and let me tell you a little story.

One day late last year, I'm at my local grocery store in Fairfield, California, looking for my customary flavored sparkling water and canned soup, when I'm stopped by an elderly gentleman who just happened to spot the t-shirt I was wearing.

"Excuse me," he said to me. "I couldn’t help but notice your shirt, there."

Looking down, I realized I was wearing a TCU shirt – one of many I attained while I went to school for my Master's degree in Journalism.

"Oh, yes," I replied.

"I was curious why you have that shirt," he asked.

"Well, actually, I went there for grad school," I explained. "I just graduated this year in Journalism."

"Oh, that’s good," he replied. "My son played football there – you might know him."

"Really?" I asked. "What’s your son’s name?"

"Jason Verrett."



I was talking to the father of arguably the best cornerback in the Big 12, if not the country.

The two-time All-Big 12 and 2013 All-American CB originally from Dominguez High School in Fairfield was the lone standout for this year’s Horned Frogs squad, who finished 4-8 in 2013 – their worst record since 1996. Despite standing at 5’9″ and dealing with a recently torn labrum, Verrett has said, himself, that he is the best corner to come out of this year’s draft.

It may not be smart to doubt him, as his accolades in the Big 12, as well as his combine and pro day showings, speak for themselves: a 4.38 40 time at the Combine (second-fastest at CB in 2014), a 39-inch vertical jump, and a bench press of 225 lbs 19 times (sixth-best at CB in 2014). Despite his small stature, he will be expected to be taken in the mid-to-late first round come Draft Day.

It would seem foolish for the 49ers, still in desperate need of a reliable, consistent secondary, NOT to look at Verrett.



Then again, the 49ers organization, over the last few years, have been anything but foolish.

They sent a scout to Verrett’s impressive pro day, and may be able to make a play for the local kid in April’s draft. His skill set, according to Niners Nation, could fit in quite well to a 49ers team in need of a nickel back – especially if they do what is expected and part ways with CB Carlos Rogers.

While he is projected to go higher than where San Francisco picks at 30th overall, the 49ers have been known to finagle deals to get the players they want. Take last year’s trade with the Cowboys to snag the 18th overall pick – standout LSU safety Eric Reid. And with a potential backfield including Verrett, Reid, Tremaine Brock and Chris Culliver, the 49ers could have the foundation for the kind of secondary that could rival the “Legion of Boom” secondary that resides with the current Super Bowl champions in the Pacific Northwest.



Additionally, Verrett could provide a buzz with the team to its fans outside of the X’s and O’s – one of the most interesting things about Verrett is his hometown story. looking back at that chance encounter with his father last year, I regret not talking further with him, and getting to know his story, as well as his son’s. It would serve him well in the local community in Fairfield, as well as the rest of the Bay Area, to delve deeper into his story. And it would make for the kind of PR that Verrett desperately needs if he is the first defensive back taken in the draft – especially if it happened to by the 49ers drafting him.

If my little story held any interest for you … well, imagine the stories if Jason Verrett became the 49ers’ franchise cornerback.
