MSR's Random Sports Vids: Vol. 1



Because it’s a lazy Friday, and because I want to share a few of these things with you, allow me to present RANDOM SPORTS VIDS!


GIF: DRAMATIC CHIPMUNK

Dun-Dun-DUUUUUNN!!!!



Let’s start with a video for the 49ers fans. Now, I remember just a couple weeks ago when people were literally scratching their heads over San Francisco’s first round pick at 30th overall.


The fact is, unless you were a bona fide draft expert – or a Northern Illinois football fan – you probably couldn’t pick SS Jimmie Ward out of a lineup. And according to various reactions by crowds and fans alike, this was likely the case.


PICTURE OF 49ERS FANS AT NFL DRAFT

"How DARE you take someone I’ve NEVER HEARD OF?! BOOOO!!!"

For guys like me, who didn’t know who Ward was, but wanted to know why he was good enough to grab at the end of the first round, you probably found YouTube videos like this one, highlighting some of his greatest attributes as a defensive player.


Fun fact: If you notice at 1:26, the play Ward makes was actually an ESPN highlight, and cracked the SportsCenter Top 10. Not really surprising, considering he simultaneously makes a play at the offensive player to prevent him from catching the ball, then, in a split second, has the wherewithal to find the ball in midair and snatch it for the interception. All at the slot corner position.


Peep this, Niners fans:


Ooh, so exciting.



Up next is something you’ve probably seen all over the internet, but couldn’t help but want to share, myself.


The fact is, we’ve all thought we were the best at novelty sports at one time in our lives. Whether it was getting the high score in “pop-a-shot” games at the arcade or ruling the playground in tetherball or pogo as a kid, you reveled in those accomplishments.


Pro athletes tend to take these novelty achievements to the next level. For example, take Bubba Watson – the 35-year-old, two-time Masters champion. Check out this trick shot, essentially hitting a putt into the PREVIOUS hole:


WARNING: RIP Headphone Users.


There are a lot of things that are cool about this video, obviously – the fact that the shot actually happened, or how cool and collected Bubba was when he hit the shot, as if he knew it was going in. Then there was the fact that, in one frame, he’s hitting the putt:


SCREENSHOT 1

"Check this out."


Then the next frame, there he is, picking up the flag just as the ball rolls into the hole:


SCREENSHOT 2

"Sup?!"


Then, there’s his little dance at the end, which is something that, I think, all of us would do if we pulled off that ridiculous putt.



Here’s another golf video that I found on the reddits, but I felt compelled to share.


It’s basically the legendary Jack Nicklaus (he of the record 18 major championships) schooling Johnny Miller on the ease of the 100-foot putt:


I love how he contends in a separate video that “the putt wasn’t that tough.” Maybe not for a golfing legend like Nicklaus. In any case, what I love about this video is what amounts to a “drop the mic” moment for Jack – he shrugs his shoulders walking away from Miller as if to say, “WAS THAT SO HARD?!”


SCREENSHOT 3

"Pfft. Rookies."


Only guys like Nicklaus can get away with a boss walk-off like that. Truly amazing.



I wanted to end this on an inspirational note – at least with this video, that I also stumbled upon on the reddits. It’s from the 2008 Indoor Track Championships. Minnesota’s Heather Dorniden literally falls on her face in the middle of the 600-meter final. What happens next is simply stunning:


She ends up WINNING THE GOTDANGED RACE.


I don’t think I would’ve believed it if I didn’t see it with my own two eyes.


Whether you’ve seen it on your random “Never Give Up” inspirational website, the feat is still amazing. Let’s face it – 99% of the time, you probably would have just seen the athlete lying there with his or her dreams of winning completely crushed. You see in this video that Dorniden wasn’t having any of that.


It truly is a story told in pictures about not giving up on your goals no matter what happens – because you never know what may come at the finish line.


…


Or, you know, you could take the point of view of all the girls Dorniden beat, and take away the fact that, no matter what you do, sometimes, you’re just not meant to win.


Man, I really wandered away from that “inspirational” kick right fast and in a hurry, didn’t I? Whoops.
