Cavs Win NBA Lottery AGAIN: Rapid Reaction

by Paulo C Camacho

May 20, 2014




Ever since LeBron James humiliated the city of Cleveland just four years ago, the NBA karmic gods have certainly been kind to their basketball team.

For the second year in a row, the Cleveland Cavaliers – who came into this year’s draft lottery with a 1.7% chance of winning – came away with the coveted Number 1 pick in June’s NBA Draft.

It was a statistical near-impossibility for the Cavaliers, who selected UNLV F Anthony Bennett in the 2013 NBA Draft, to land the top spot again – after all, the previous two times the burgundy-and-gold won the NBA Draft Lottery, Dan Gilbert’s son Nick was the team’s representative. This time around, it was new GM David Griffin. In any case, the fates were kind to the franchise, and will have their pick of the talented litter.

Meanwhile, the Milwaukee Bucks, who sported the league’s worst record, will be picking second, and the Philadelphia 76ers will be picking third. Most likely, these three teams will pick up top prospects Jabari Parker of Duke, and Andrew Wiggins and Joel Embiid of Kansas. The only question is in what order they will fly off the board.

Despite what people think of the Cavaliers – who have wallowed in lottery hell since “The Decision” of 2010, but have won the lottery for the third time in the four years since James left Cleveland – or their luck, they find themselves as one of the most charmed franchises in the league.

Other notes:

  • Both the Boston Celtics and the Los Angeles Lakers – the two franchises with the most championships in NBA History, with 17 and 16 NBA titles, respectively – found themselves with single-digit lottery picks for the first time since 1966. It was only the third time the Lakers were in the lottery – an extremely rare occurrence – and the twelfth time the Celtics have been in the lottery. Their bad luck put them both out of the top 5, however – the Celtics will be picking sixth, and the Lakers seventh.

  • The New York Knicks’ personnel woes continue, as they were not represented in the NBA Draft Lottery this year. Their pick was either going to the Denver Nuggets or Orlando Magic, depending on where the pick fell, as a result of the Carmelo Anthony trade of 2011. In this case, it went to Orlando. It should be noted that they also don’t have a second-round pick, so they will not be partaking in the talent pool for 2014 – another tough blow to a franchise with little options to improve going into next season.

  • Speaking of the Magic, Orlando will be one of two teams picking twice in the top 14, with the fourth and twelfth picks. the 76ers, with the third and tenth picks (the tenth coming from the Pelicans), are the other. Turns out that, despite not landing the top overall pick, their dismal 19-63 record was worth it, after all.

  • As for my hometown team, the Sacramento Kings, I found out this little tidbit from ESPN.com – they find themselves in the draft lottery for the eighth year in a row – the longest current streak in the NBA. What’s worse – they were knocked down a peg from their default spot at seventh overall when the Cavaliers leapfrogged them in the lottery. There are still options for them – likely players like Indiana’s Noah Vonleh, Oklahoma State’s Marcus Smart and Creighton’s Doug McDermott will be available – but there may not be a bona fide superstar to take there. And, unless they make a trade in the next month, it will be the only pick they have in this year’s draft.

  • On a side note, there were two draft lottery representatives that stood out above the rest – and, no, I’m not talking about Julius Irving and James Worthy.

    • As I’m sure many lady-lovers in the television audience likely noticed, two franchises were represented by the daughters of their respective owners – the Sacramento Kings sent 20-year-old Anjali Ranadive, daughter to majority owner Vivek Ranadive, while the Milwaukee Bucks sent 18-year-old Mallory Edens, daughter to owner Wes Edens. It was hard to miss amid all the hoopla of the draft lottery. With the exposure both young ladies received from the event, I wouldn’t be surprised to see either one in the blogosphere for weeks, months, or years to come.
